# Privacy policy

> A privacy policy tells visitors what personal data you collect, why, on what legal basis, who you share it with, how long you keep it, and what rights they have. Under the LGPD, it must be clear, accessible, and in Portuguese for Brazilian users.

## What it is

A privacy policy is the public document that explains how your website handles personal data. Under the GDPR it satisfies the transparency obligations of Articles 13 and 14. Under Brazil's LGPD (Lei 13.709/2018), it satisfies the principles of transparency (Art. 6, VI) and free access (Art. 6, IV), ensuring data subjects can consult how their data is processed.

The LGPD applies to any processing of personal data conducted in Brazil, targeting individuals in Brazil, or involving data collected in Brazil — regardless of where the organization is located. Since February 2026, the ANPD operates as a fully independent regulatory agency (Resolution 33/2026) with sector-specific enforcement capacity.

## Why it matters

If you collect any personal data — names, email addresses, IP addresses, cookies tied to a user, form submissions — you owe the visitor an accurate description of what happens to it. Both GDPR and LGPD treat a missing, vague, or out-of-date policy as a transparency failure.

Under the LGPD, the ANPD's 2026–2027 enforcement priorities explicitly target data subject rights compliance. Fines reach up to 2% of annual revenue in Brazil, capped at R$50 million per infraction, plus non-monetary sanctions including public disclosure, data blocking, and processing suspension.

## How to implement

A privacy policy must disclose, at minimum:

### Identity and contact

- **Identity of the controller** (controlador), including postal address and a contact for privacy questions.
- **Data Protection Officer (Encarregado)** — LGPD requires controllers to appoint a DPO (Art. 41). Name them and provide a public contact channel. Small-scale agents (revenue ≤ R$4.8M) are exempt unless processing high-risk data.

### Data collected and purposes

- **Categories of personal data** processed — account data, usage data, payment data, contact data, technical identifiers (IP addresses, device IDs).
- **Sensitive data** — if you process racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, political opinions, health data, biometric data, or genetic data, disclose this separately. LGPD Art. 11 requires explicit consent or a specific legal exception.
- **Purposes** for each category — running the service, billing, marketing, analytics, security, legal compliance.

### Legal basis

- **Lawful basis** for each purpose. LGPD Art. 7 provides **ten legal bases** (four more than GDPR):
  1. Consent
  2. Legal/regulatory obligation
  3. Public administration
  4. Research purposes
  5. Contract performance
  6. Exercise of legal rights
  7. Life or physical safety
  8. Health protection
  9. Legitimate interests
  10. Credit protection

- If relying on legitimate interests, document the three-part assessment: legitimacy, necessity, and balancing against data subject rights.
- If relying on consent, state that it must be freely given, informed, specific, and revocable at any time without penalty.

### Sharing and international transfers

- **Recipients and processors** — payment providers, hosting, email, analytics, customer support tools. List by name or category.
- **International transfers** — Since January 2026, the Brazil-EU mutual adequacy decision (Resolution CD/ANPD No. 32/2026) allows data to flow directly between Brazil and the EU/EEA without additional safeguards. For transfers to other countries, use ANPD-approved Standard Contractual Clauses (Resolution 19/2024, mandatory since August 2025), specific highlighted consent, or ANPD authorization.

### Retention and rights

- **Retention periods** for each category, or the criteria used to determine them. Indefinite retention violates the necessity principle.
- **Data subject rights** under LGPD Art. 18:
  - Confirmation and access (immediate simplified; detailed within **15 days**)
  - Correction of incomplete/inaccurate data
  - Anonymization, blocking, or deletion of unnecessary data
  - Data portability in structured, interoperable format
  - Information on entities data was shared with
  - Information on the possibility of denying consent and its consequences
  - Consent revocation at any time
  - Right to petition the ANPD
  - Right to review automated decisions (Art. 20)
- Under GDPR, also include: right to restriction, right to object, and right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

### Additional disclosures

- **Cookies and tracking** — either inline or by linking to a separate cookie notice. LGPD requires opt-in consent for non-essential cookies.
- **Children and adolescents** — if you process minors' data, disclose this. LGPD Art. 14 requires processing in the "best interest" of the child, with parental/guardian consent. The ECA Digital (2025) adds further obligations for online platforms.
- **Automated decisions** — if you use profiling or automated decision-making, disclose this per LGPD Art. 20. Data subjects have the right to request review of decisions made solely by automated processing.
- **Incident notification** — state that in case of a security incident with potential relevant risk, the ANPD and affected data subjects will be notified within **3 business days** (Resolution CD/ANPD No. 15/2024).
- **A "last updated" date** visible at the top of the page.

### Accessibility

- Link to the policy from the footer of every page. Do not put it behind a login.
- For Brazilian users, provide the policy **in Portuguese**.
- Use clear, plain language — LGPD's transparency principle requires information to be accessible to the general public.

## Common mistakes

- Copy-pasted boilerplate that mentions practices you do not have, or omits ones you do.
- Listing only GDPR's 6 legal bases when you serve Brazilian users (LGPD has 10).
- A single "we may share data with partners" line that names no one.
- No retention information at all.
- Hiding the policy in the terms of service.
- Claiming "no international transfers" while using US-based SaaS tools.
- Not naming the DPO/Encarregado or providing a contact channel.
- Stating a 30-day DSAR response time without noting LGPD's 15-day requirement.
- Updating the document without changing the date — or changing the date without telling existing users.
- Not disclosing automated decision-making when using AI/ML for profiling.
